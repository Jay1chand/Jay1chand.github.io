PWM Modulation Simulation

Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) is a modulation technique where the width of pulses is varied in proportion to the amplitude of the input signal. It is widely used in power electronics, motor control, and communication systems.

  • fs: Sampling frequency used for digital simulation.
  • f_pwm: Frequency of the PWM signal (carrier).
  • Duration: Total time for simulation (in seconds).
  • Seed: Random seed for reproducibility.
